Why this matters

Governments around the world spend over $9.5 trillion each year buying goods and services through public contracts.

But citizens are denied access to information about who gets this money, how it was awarded, what was agreed and what it buys for them.

Without proper transparency and accountability, vast sums of public money will continue to be lost to fraud, corruption, overcharging, and under-delivery by private contractors.

People everywhere should be able to see where public money goes and what it buys to enable essential public interest advocacy, reporting and reforms.
